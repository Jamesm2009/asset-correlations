📊 What is this dashboard?

The Asset Correlation Matrix shows how closely 44 ETFs move together over a selected time window, covering US equities, sectors, international markets, bonds, commodities, and currencies.

Correlation ranges from -1.0 (perfect inverse) to +1.0 (perfectly in lockstep). Near 0 means the two assets move independently.

🎨 Reading the colour scale

-1.0 strong inverse
-0.5
0.0 no relationship
+0.5
+1.0 strong positive

Dark red = assets moving closely together. Dark blue = moving in opposite directions. White = little relationship.

📅 Time windows

  • 1 Month (21 trading days) — medium-term; most reliable for portfolio decisions.
  • 1 Week (5 trading days) — very short-term; sensitive to recent events, changes rapidly.

🔍 Compare Two ETFs tab

Type any two ticker symbols to instantly see their rolling 21-day correlation over the full 91-day data window, even if they are not in the main 44-ticker list (as long as the data is available).

💰 Practical uses

  • Diversification: Low or negative correlation between holdings reduces portfolio volatility.
  • Concentration risk: High correlation (+0.8+) means you may be less diversified than you think.
  • Hedging: GLD and TLT often negatively correlate with equities in risk-off environments.
  • Regime detection: When correlations across many pairs suddenly rise, the market is typically in a stress/risk-off regime.

⏰ Data and refresh

Prices come from Yahoo Finance via yfinance. The dashboard uses 91 trading days of daily closes, with weekends and holidays forward-filled. Data is cached in Upstash Redis and refreshed nightly via cron. Click Refresh Data in the header to force an immediate update after market close.
